Manchester City defeated Manchester United 2-1:Ilya Gundogan secured the FA Cup:kept the triple-threat alive.

Manchester City defeated rival Manchester United 2-1 at Wembley to win the 2023 FA Cup and move one victory away from completing a historic Triple. Ilkay Gundogan scored twice in the victory.

Gundogan scored the quickest cup final goal in history after only 13 seconds as Pep Guardiola's Premier League champions seized the lead right from the kickoff. Long stretches of Citizens dominance were broken by a VAR intervention that resulted in a penalty being given for a hard handball against Jack Grealish, just beyond the half-hour mark.

The Red Devils were fortunate to be tied going into halftime thanks to United captain Bruno Fernandes, who calmly and quickly leveled the score. After the break, the match felt more equal, but City regained control on minute 51 when Gundogan volleyed another shot past a half blind David De Gea from 25 yards. Marcus Rashford, Alejandro Garnacho, a replacement, and Raphael Varane all came close to tying the score for United, but they were unable to score to force extra time as City won the domestic double. In an effort to win Europe's top prize for the first time in their history, City will now focus on the June 10 Champions League final versus Inter Milan.

MANCHESTER BLUE REINS SUPREME AT WEMBLEY

This was the first all-Manchester final in FA Cup history, and the blue side of the city will never forget it. In addition to securing the club's second domestic Double, a seventh FA Cup also prevented Erik ten Hag from completing a cup double that would have confirmed the notion that The Red Devils had acquired significant momentum from his first season as manager. The wider picture is that it may, of course, also be the second leg of a legendary Treble for City, which would replicate United's exceptional accomplishment in 1999 and is another reason why the team from Old Trafford was so anxious to win this Wembley matchup.

After the final whistle, while the Man City players were celebrating, the TV mics picked up Erling Haaland telling his teammates, "One more, just one more." They are aware that something so exceptional and uncommon is about to happen. Guardiola joined City in order to win the Champions League, and after years of disappointment, Haaland was widely seen as the final missing piece needed to hoist that coveted trophy. Although they have come close in the past, it is difficult to imagine anyone stopping this City squad now, especially with Inter Milan standing in their way the following Saturday night.
