The Establishment of Diplomatic Relations between Japan and Nepal

With the establishment of diplomatic relations on September 1, 1956, relations expanded into many areas. The Japanese Ambassador in New Delhi was also appointed Ambassador to Nepal before the opening of embassies in Kathmandu and Tokyo. Similarly, the Nepalese ambassador to New Delhi also served as ambassador to Japan. Thus, His Excellency Seijiro Yoshizawa and His Excellency Daman Shumsha Rana were appointed as the first Ambassadors of their respective countries.

Ambassador Yoshizawa presenting his credentials to King Mahendra (September 22, 1956)

First residential Ambassador Kira presenting his credentials to King Mahendra (February 26, 1968)

The permanent embassy of Nepal was opened in his July 1965 in Tokyo. Mr. Bharat Raj Rajbandari was appointed Ambassador to Japan. The Japanese Embassy in Kathmandu was opened in February 1968. Hidemichi Kira was appointed Ambassador to Nepal.

A visit by the Japanese imperial family and the Nepalese royal family in 1960 cemented the relationship even further. In April 1960, Their Majesties King Mahendra and Queen Ratna visited Japan for the first time as state guests. visited Nepal on behalf of Empress of Japan. His Majesty King Birendra (then Crown Prince) spent several months at the University of Tokyo in 1967 as a special student. This visit was a good opportunity to establish a close personal friendship with the Japanese imperial family. Their Majesties visited Nepal again in 1975 to attend the coronation of His Majesty King Birendra. In the years that followed, members of the Japanese imperial family and Nepalese royal family visited occasionally.
