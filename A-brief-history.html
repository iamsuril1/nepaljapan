A Brief History of Japan-Nepal Relations

The cultural relations between Nepal and Japan trace back far further than the 1899 visit of Reverend Ekai Kawaguchi, a Japanese Zen Buddhist pundit and ascetic on his way to Tibet. It is clear that ancient Japanese intellectuals who read a travelogue published 1300 years ago by Xuan Jiang, a great Chinese monk and philosopher of the Tang Dynasty, would have came across a description of the old Kathmandu valley during the time of King Amsuvarman of the Licchavi Dynasty.

Fig:Ekai Kawaguchi

It goes without saying that Japan and Nepal have a lot in common in terms of culture, religion, and even feeling. Many of the deities worshiped by the Japanese are also Nepalese deities, which will surprise many people. Nepalese and Japanese traditional traditions are inextricably connected by a subterranean stream of history that pervades both of our societies. Furthermore, Nepal has traditionally drawn Japanese visitors with its lush, verdant foliage, as well as its wonderfully diverse panoramas, each with its own flora and fauna. It should also be remembered that the first students sent overseas by Chandra Shamsher Rana, Nepal's then-prime minister, in 1902 were those who went to Japan.

The post World War Two period witnessed the rapid strengthening of this friendship at different levels. Since 1952, the Himalayas have lured waves of Japanese mountaineering teams. Diplomatic relations were established on September 1, 1956, ensued by the opening of the Royal Nepalese Embassy in Tokyo in 1956, and the Japanese Embassy in Kathmandu in 1968. Another chapter of our cordial relationship was opened by the exchange of Royal visits and high-level official interactions of both our countries. Their Majesties King Mahendra and Queen Ratna visited Japan in 1960. In the same year, Crown Prince Akihito and Princess Michiko (present Emperor and Empress) visited this country. The Imperial couple visited twice. The visit of the late Royal Highness, Crown Prince Dipendra, to Japan still lingers in the hearts of the Japanese people. The recent exchange of visits made between the prime ministers of both countries, namely Mr. G.P. Koirala and Mr. Mori, was another milestone in the history of friendly relations between our two countries.
